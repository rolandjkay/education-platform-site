* So, we need some formula that can represent any process where the rate at which the number of things increases is proportional to number of things that there are.

* Show that with bacteria we have 

 Time      Number of bacteria    2^x
 ----      ------------------    ---
  0         1                    2^0
  1         2                    2^1
  2         4                    2^2
  3         8                    2^3 
  4         16
  5         32
  6         64
  7         128
  8         256
  9         512
  10       1024

So, for bacteria splitting into two the number of bacteria is given by $n = 2 ^ x$. You can easily convince yourself that a 
bacteria that splits in three would develop as $n = 3 ^ x$.

So, probably, we want a formula like $n = a^x$, where $a$ is related to how fast the process develops at each time step.

What is the constant of proportionality for the bacteria population:

n = 2^x  

ln n = x in 2
1/n dn/dx = ln 2
dn/dx = (ln 2)n  

So, the constant of proportionality between the rate of change of $n$ and $n$ is $ln 2$.

More, generally, the constant of proportionality for $n = a^x$ is $ln a$.

So, setting $a=e$ gives an expression where the constant of proportionlity is $e$.

In otherwords, in the same way as the value of $\pi$ arise from observing the area of a circle, $e$ arises by observing the value that must be raised to the power of x to achieve a process where the constant of proportionality is 1.0



You can see this property in action with the diagram below. For any base $a$, the exponential $y = a^x$ has slope proportional to its value: $\frac{dy}{dx} = k \cdot y$, where $k = \ln(a)$. The grey curve is $y = e^x$ with $k = 1$. Drag the slider to match the red curve to the grey — that's the moment you've found Euler's number.
