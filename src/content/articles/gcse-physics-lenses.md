Lenses are one of the most important topics in GCSE Physics and appear across cameras, microscopes, telescopes, and even your own eyes.

## How a convex lens works

A convex (converging) lens bends parallel light rays so they meet at a single point called the **focal point**. The distance from the lens to the focal point is the **focal length** (f).

### The thin lens equation

$$\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u}$$

Where:
- **f** = focal length
- **v** = image distance (from lens to image)
- **u** = object distance (from lens to object)

### Ray diagram rules

1. A ray parallel to the principal axis refracts through the focal point
2. A ray through the optical centre passes straight through
3. A ray through the focal point refracts parallel to the principal axis

## Real and virtual images

| Object position | Image type | Image position |
|---|---|---|
| Beyond 2f | Real, inverted, smaller | Between f and 2f |
| At 2f | Real, inverted, same size | At 2f |
| Between f and 2f | Real, inverted, larger | Beyond 2f |
| At f | No image formed | — |
| Inside f | Virtual, upright, larger | Same side as object |
