Making a music video is one of the best first projects for a young filmmaker. You already have the soundtrack — so you can focus entirely on the visuals.

## What you'll need

- A camera (a smartphone works great)
- A song you love (get permission if it's not yours)
- A location or two
- A friend to help (optional but useful)
- Free editing software: DaVinci Resolve or CapCut

## Step 1: Listen and visualise

Play the track 5–10 times. What images come to mind? Write them down without judging them. Pay attention to:

- **Verse** — story, character, setting
- **Chorus** — energy, emotion, movement
- **Bridge** — contrast, twist, reflection

## Step 2: Plan your shots

Create a simple shot list. You don't need a full storyboard, but write down at least:

1. Where you'll film each section
2. What the camera will be doing (static, pan, handheld, close-up)
3. What the subject will be doing

## Step 3: Shoot to the beat

Play the song on a speaker while you film. This makes editing much easier — you'll naturally move and cut in time with the music.

**Pro tip:** Film more than you think you need. Aim for 3× as much footage as the song is long.

## Step 4: Edit

Import your footage and the audio track. Start by placing clips in order, then tighten cuts so they fall on the beat. Add colour grading last.

## Share it

Post it, show it to friends, submit it to the school's project showcase. Every video you make teaches you something the next one will benefit from.
