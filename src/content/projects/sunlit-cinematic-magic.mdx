Summer is here, and it's the perfect time to get outside and start experimenting with your photography and filmmaking skills. Whether you're using a phone or a camera, the sun can be your best friend for creating stunning, cinematic footage. Let's dive into some tips and tricks to make the most out of natural sunlight and elevate your visual content.

## Embrace the Sunlight

Most of the time, when we take photos or shoot videos, we try to avoid direct sunlight hitting our lens. That's why professional cameras often have a triangular box, called a **matte box**, on the front. The photo camera you use to take holiday snaps has a **lens hood** instead.

The lens hood helps to prevent unwanted light from causing flares and washed-out images. However, removing it and allowing the sun to shine directly into the lens can create some amazing effects that add a dreamy, cinematic quality to your footage.

## The Beauty of Lens Flares

When you let sunlight enter your lens directly, you introduce **lens flares** — those beautiful streaks and bursts of light that add a touch of magic to your images. These flares result from sunlight bouncing around inside the lens, and they can give your footage a romantic, ethereal look. People often spend a lot of time trying to recreate these kinds of effects in post-production using filters, but you can get this with very little effort if you aim to shoot it directly, "in camera".

## Tips for Getting the Best Effects

**Don't clean your lens:** This might sound counterintuitive, but a bit of dirt and imperfections on your lens can enhance the organic feel of your footage. Tiny particles and smudges can create interesting flares and textures.

**Use creative filters:** If you want a soft, hazy look, try using Promist filters. They diffuse the light and create a gentle, dreamy effect.

**DIY filters:** No Promist filters? No problem! Try rubbing Vaseline onto a sheet of clear plastic and holding it in front of the lens. Or, tie a thin stocking over your lens or wrap it around your phone's camera to soften the image.

## Get Creative During the Holidays

Now that the summer holidays are here, it's the perfect time to grab your phone or camera and start experimenting. Step outside, find a sunny spot, and let your creativity flow. Here are a few ideas:

**Golden hour shots:** Capture the magic during the golden hour — the first hour after sunrise and the last hour before sunset. The sunlight is softer and creates beautiful, warm tones.

**Backlit portraits:** Position your subject with the sun behind them. This creates a halo effect and adds depth to your photos.

**Look for nature:** Especially in summer, flowers, interesting-looking trees, or sunlight filtering through leaves can make great subjects.

## Important Health & Safety Tips

While experimenting with sunlight can be fun and rewarding, it's crucial to stay safe:

- ⚠️ **NEVER** look directly at the sun through the viewfinder of a camera. This can cause severe and permanent eye damage.
- ⚠️ Avoid filming the sun with cameras you don't want to risk damaging. The intense light can harm your equipment. If you're worried, try filming sunrises and sunsets instead, as the sunlight is less intense.

## Get out there!

Summer offers endless opportunities to explore and enhance your photography skills. By using the sun as your light source and experimenting with lens flares and creative effects, you can create unique, cinematic footage that stands out. Grab your gear, head outside, and start capturing those summer vibes — the best shots often come from having fun and embracing the unexpected!
