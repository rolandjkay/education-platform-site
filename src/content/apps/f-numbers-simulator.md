Explore how changing the aperture and focal length of a camera lens affects which parts of a scene are in focus.

## What this simulator shows

- How light rays converge through a lens system
- The effect of f-number on depth of field
- How focal length changes magnification and perspective

## Controls

- **Orbit**: Click and drag to rotate the view
- **Aperture slider**: Change the f-number and watch the depth of field change
- **Focal length slider**: Adjust focal length to see how it affects ray paths

## Background

The **f-number** (or f-stop) is the ratio of the lens focal length to the diameter of the entrance pupil:

**N = f / D**

A low f-number (e.g. f/1.4) means a wide aperture, letting in lots of light but producing a shallow depth of field. A high f-number (e.g. f/16) gives a deep depth of field but requires more light or a longer exposure.
